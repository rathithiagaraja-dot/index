Raja Raja Chola

Introduction

Raja Raja Chola I, born as Arulmozhi Varman, was one of the greatest kings of South India. He ruled the Chola Empire from 985 CE to 1014 CE and transformed it into a powerful maritime empire.

Early Life

Raja Raja Chola was born into the Chola royal family and was known for his bravery, intelligence, and leadership skills from a young age.

Major Achievements

  • Expanded the Chola Empire across South India.
  • Conquered large parts of Sri Lanka.
  • Built a powerful naval force.
  • Captured the Maldives and Lakshadweep Islands.
  • Improved administration and revenue systems.
  • Built the magnificent Brihadisvara Temple in Thanjavur.




Brihadisvara Temple

Built in 1010 CE, the Brihadisvara Temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the finest examples of Dravidian architecture.

Legacy

Raja Raja Chola's reign marked the golden age of the Chola Empire. His contributions to architecture, administration, and military expansion continue to be celebrated today.
